Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba

Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba, "Blade of Demon Destruction", is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Koyoharu Gotouge. It follows teenage Tanjiro Kamado, who strives to become a demon slayer after his family was slaughtered and his younger sister, Nezuko, turned into a demon.

A 26-episode anime television series adaption produced by Ufotable aired from April to September 2019. A sequel film, Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba - The Movie: Mugen Train, was released in October 2020 and became the highest-grossing anime film and Japanese film of all time. An 18-episode second season of the anime series aired from October 2021 to February 2022. It featured one original episode, re-edited the Mugan Train film into six episodes, and then covered the "Entertainment District" arc from the manga in 11 episodes. A third season covering the "Swordsmith Village" arc is set to premiere in April 2023.

As of February 2021, the manga had over 150 million copies in circulation, including digital versions, making it the ninth best-selling manga in 2019 and 2020. Both the manga and its anime adaption have received critical acclaim. The anime series has received numerous awards and is considered one of the best anime of the 2019s. In 2020, the Demon Slayer franchise generated an estimated annual sales revenue of ¥1 trillion ($8.75 billion), becoming one of the highest-grossing media franchise of all time.
